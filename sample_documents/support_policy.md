# Support Escalation Policy

Customer support tickets are triaged by severity and business impact. Severity 1 incidents include full service outages, confirmed data loss, or security incidents affecting active users. Severity 2 incidents include major feature failures with a workaround. Severity 3 incidents include usability issues, minor defects, or configuration questions.

Severity 1 tickets must receive an initial response within 30 minutes and must be escalated to the incident commander immediately. Severity 2 tickets must receive an initial response within 4 business hours. Severity 3 tickets must receive an initial response within 2 business days.

Support agents should collect the customer account ID, affected workflow, timestamps, screenshots, logs, and the business impact before escalating. Escalations without a reproducible description should be returned to the support queue for clarification unless the customer reports a live outage.

The support team maintains a customer-facing incident update every 60 minutes during a Severity 1 incident. Updates must include current status, known workaround, owner, and next update time. When the incident is resolved, the final message should include the resolution summary and whether a post-incident review will be published.

Refund requests are not handled by support unless they are connected to a confirmed service incident. Support may create a billing review case, but the finance team owns approval.
