# Security Incident Runbook

A security incident is any confirmed or suspected event that threatens confidentiality, integrity, or availability of customer data or production systems. Examples include leaked credentials, malware on an employee device, suspicious administrator activity, or unauthorized data access.

The first responder must preserve evidence, rotate exposed credentials, and notify the security lead within 15 minutes. If customer data may be affected, legal and communications teams must be notified within one hour.

Incident severity is assigned by the security lead. Critical incidents require a dedicated incident channel, an incident commander, and a written timeline. The incident commander owns status updates, decision logs, and closure criteria.

External customer notification is required when there is confirmed unauthorized access to customer data. The notification must describe what happened, what information was involved, what action was taken, and what customers can do to protect themselves.

Post-incident reviews are required for critical and high incidents. The review must include root cause, contributing factors, customer impact, remediation tasks, owners, and target dates.
